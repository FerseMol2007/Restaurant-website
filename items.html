Parathi is a popular layered flatbread that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is widely enjoyed in India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nepal as a breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snack.

Dosa is a thin, crispy, and savory pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and black gram (urad dal). It is one of the most popular traditional dishes in South India and is widely enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Parotta is a soft, flaky, and layered flatbread that is especially popular in Tamil Nadu and Kerala. It is a favorite street food and restaurant dish, commonly enjoyed for lunch or dinner.
